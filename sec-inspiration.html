Inspiration

~ A few examples of photographers that inspire us ~

United States

Ansel Adams

(1902 - 1984)

Was an American photographer and environmentalist, best known for his black-and-white photographs of the American West. Adams primarily used large-format cameras despite their size, weight, setup time, and film cost, because their high resolution helped ensure sharpness in his images.

United States

Robert Capa

(1913 - 1954)

He is considered by some to be the greatest combat and adventure photographer in history. During his career he risked his life numerous times, most dramatically as the only civilian photographer landing on Omaha Beach on D-Day. He documented the course of World War II.

France

Henri Cartier-Bresson

(1908 - 2004)

He acquired the Leica camera with 50 mm lens in Marseilles that would accompany him for many years. The anonymity that the small camera gave him in a crowd or during an intimate moment was essential in overcoming the formal and unnatural behavior of those who were aware of being photographed. He enhanced his anonymity by painting all shiny parts of the Leica with black paint.

United States

Dorothea Lange

(1895 - 1965)

She was an American documentary photographer, who studied photography at Columbia University and took a photographic trip around the world in 1918. When she ran out of funds in San Francisco, she remained, opened a photographic studio, and during the early 1930s began photographing homeless rural people flooding into the city from the Dust Bowl exodus.
